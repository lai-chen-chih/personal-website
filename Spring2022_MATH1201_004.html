Course Information

Topics: Vectors and the geometry of space, vector functions, functions of several variables and partial derivatives

Prerequisites: Math UN1101: Calculus I or an equivalent. For more information on calculus: http://www.math.columbia.edu/programs-math/undergraduate-program/calculus-classes/

  • There will be 11 weekly homework assignments.

  • Make sure the writing in your submission is clear enough to the reader.
  • The lowest homework grade will be dropped.
  • Late homework will not be accepted under any circumstances. If you have a medical circumstance that prevents you from turning in homework, you will need a doctor's note to receive an extension.

Exams

  • There will be two midterm tests and one cumulative final exam.
  • The midterm tests will be taken during class time. The topics covered by each of the two tests will be announced in class and posted on the course website. If you are unable to take one of the midterms at the scheduled time, please let me know as soon as possible so that we can figure out a solution.
  • The final exam is provisionally scheduled by the University. The final exam is cumulative and covers all the material discussed during the semester.
  • You may use a formula sheet during the exams, no longer than two sides of an A4 page.
  • The only electronic device you may use is a calculator (e.g. scientific calculator), but programmable or graphing calculators are not allowed.
