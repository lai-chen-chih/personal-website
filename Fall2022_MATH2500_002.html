  • Welcome to Analysis and Optimization (Section 002)! This is a one-semester course in mathematical analysis of continuous optimization, including unconstrained and contrained problems.

Course Information

Topics: Mathematical methods for economics. Quadratic forms, Hessian, implicit functions. Convex sets, convex functions. Optimization, constrained optimization, Kuhn-Tucker conditions. Elements of the calculus of variations and optimal control

Prerequisites: MATH UN1102 and MATH UN1201 or the equivalent and MATH UN2010

Homework

  • There will biweekly homework assignments.
  • Collaboration and discussion with your classmates is encouraged, but I encourage you to attempt the problems on your own before you discuss them with classmates, and you must write up assignments individually.
  • Make sure the writing in your submission is clear enough to the reader.
  • The lowest homework grade will be dropped.
  • Late homework will not be accepted under any circumstances. If you have a medical circumstance that prevents you from turning in homework, you will need a doctor's note to receive an extension.

Exams

  • There will be two midterm tests and one cumulative final exam.
  • The midterm tests will be taken during class time. The topics covered by each of the two tests will be announced in class and posted on the course website. If you are unable to take one of the midterms at the scheduled time, please let me know as soon as possible so that we can figure out a solution.
